The Preface

It was almost like any other cold, misty night. Except, it wasn’t.

Nothing about this night was like any other.

While the same crescent fold of the moon bestowed upon me, the location was different. I was cast in the city that never sleeps, cast among masses of different people.

Instead of the suburban lighting that I’d typically receive, I was showered with illuminating billboards and signs. I was dumbfounded by the street shows occurring, by the environment itself. As I walked along every corner, I became more and more convinced that this was a land of its own.

Of course, back then I was 12, and oh so proud to label myself as a pre-teen; it is easy to see why New York City amazed me.

But the amazement, the enthrallment, was short-lived; as any ordinary person could imagine reality isn’t all rainbows and giggles. No, unfortunately, we do not live in a society that only nurtures playful atmospheres; no, we live in a society that is jaded.

Adults passed left and right of me, a quickly saddening little girl.

When I visited New York City, I wasn’t just mesmerized by the city itself, no, I was stricken by the individuals at Times Square. The realization that people inhabited the streets of the supposedly gold-paved streets of America shocked me.

Where was the American Dream? A land, I only previously thought was a safe haven. I had made my way here with hopes of a better for myself and my family, but I quickly caught on to the reality around me.

The world is cruel. People are unfair. And, well insurance issues and political polarization are all too common problems that many of us face, citizens or not.

It was only till later that I discovered the mechanism behind the “American Savior”.

Oh silly me, how could I have not seen the red flags?
